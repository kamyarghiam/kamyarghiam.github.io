I like to watch things grow.

I like to plant an idea and watch it spread to many people. That's why I studied business administration, to communicate my ideas effectively and convince people of what's important. I studied computer science to give me the tools that spread my ideas and help others. During my time there, I was a teaching assistant (TA) for 7 semesters across several classes. In particular, I was the head TA for the undergraduate parallel algorithms course in my final year. I also did two internships at Amazon. In the second summer at Amazon, I was on the Advertising Translation Team, and my project saved Amazon ~$700,000 in annual costs by integrating NLP models into their advertisement translation workflow. I also worked on/at 4 different startup projects in college. Two of them were education startups that helped high school students learn or perform research in computer science.

After college (June 2021), I joined Kalshi as the 15th full-time employee. While at Kalshi, I designed and implemented the first versions of the referrals program and tax reporting pages. I also collaborated on developing the market ops panel, which is an internal tool that allows the contracts team to list markets on Kalshi's website. Finally, I spent the majority of my time there working on a project called immediate funding. This project allows members of Kalshi to receive a portion of their deposits immediately (rather than waiting several days for ACH funds to land).


As of November 2022, I joined Vatic Investments as a software engineer.
