About Me

I like to watch things grow. I like to plant an idea and watch it spread to many people. That's why I study business administration, to communicate my ideas effectively and convince people of what's important. I study computer science to structure my problem solving approach. Two years ago, I came into Carnegie Mellon with no computer science knowledge. Within one semester, I was teaching one of the most popular computer programming courses on campus. Within two semesters, I had a technical internship in London. Within three semesters, I was working as a machine learning engineer at the Harvard Innovation Labs. And finally, within four semesters, I had an internship at the largest e-commerce company in the world, Amazon. This semester, I'm giving back to the community. I'm working on a project to create location-based requests (email me for more information!). I want to make the most of my last four semesters at college. This summer will shape the rest of my career. I look forward to seeing what we can do together!
