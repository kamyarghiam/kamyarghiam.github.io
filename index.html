I like to watch things grow.

I like to plant an idea and watch it spread to many people. That's why I studied business administration, to communicate my ideas effectively and convince people of what's important. I studied computer science to give me the tools that spread my ideas and help others. During my time at CMU, I was a teaching assistant for several courses, and I worked at a few ed-tech startups. Later, I did two internships at Amazon. In the second summer at Amazon, I was on the Advertising Translation Team, and my project saved Amazon ~$700,000 in annual costs by integrating NLP models into their advertisement translation workflow.

After college (June 2021), I joined Kalshi as the 15th full-time employee. While at Kalshi, I designed and implemented the first versions of the referrals program and tax reporting pages. I also collaborated on developing the market ops panel, which is an internal tool that allows the contracts team to list markets on Kalshi's website. Finally, I spent the majority of my time there working on a project called immediate funding. This project allows members of Kalshi to receive a portion of their deposits immediately (rather than waiting several days for ACH funds to land).


As of November 2022, I joined Vatic Investments as a software engineer.
